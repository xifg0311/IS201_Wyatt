Freediving

What is Freediving?

Freediving is a form of underwater diving that relies on breath-holding rather than the use of scuba equipment. It has been practiced for centuries for activities such as fishing, gathering, and exploration, and today it is also a recreational and competitive sport. Freediving emphasizes relaxation, breath control, and awareness of the body and surrounding environment.

I became interested in freediving because of the sense of freedom it offers underwater. In 2023, I earned my AIDA 2 freediving certification in Taiwan. What I enjoy most is the ability to move freely in the water while observing marine life and underwater landscapes. Being immersed in the ocean and its natural environment is something I find genuinely fascinating.

What Freediving Involves

  1. Physical Skills
    • Breath control and breath-holding techniques
    • Body positioning and movement in water
    • Equalization to adapt to changes in pressure
  2. Mental Awareness
    • Staying calm and relaxed underwater
    • Managing stress and conserving energy
    • Maintaining focus and self-awareness
